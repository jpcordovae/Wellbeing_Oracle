# Wellbeing Oracle

**Know how your people are really doing — before fatigue becomes an incident.**

Wellbeing Oracle is a workplace wellbeing platform that runs quietly on a smartwatch and companion phone app. It reads everyday signals from the body — heart rhythm, movement, sleep patterns, and more — and turns them into a clear, continuous picture of strain and recovery across three dimensions that matter at work: **physical capacity**, **mental load**, and **social support**.

No daily surveys. No extra tasks for frontline teams. No guesswork for HR.

---

## Built for organisations that cannot afford to be surprised

If your workforce operates in safety-critical, high-consequence, or always-on environments, you already know the pattern: the sick note arrives after the damage is done. The near-miss report surfaces weeks too late. Pulse surveys go unanswered because people are too tired — or too wary — to respond honestly.

Wellbeing Oracle is designed for **employers, HR leaders, and occupational health teams** who need earlier, more honest signal than annual medicals, employee assistance programmes, or generic “recovery scores” can provide.

| Typical gap | What Wellbeing Oracle adds |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Self-referral only, often after crisis | **Continuous, passive** insight — nothing new for operators to fill in |
| Surveys that field teams ignore | **Always-on** understanding drawn from signals people already generate |
| Body-only wearable scores | **Three connected views** — body, mind, and social context together |
| One bad day mistaken for a trend | **Multi-week memory** — separates a rough shift from sustained strain |
| Individual surveillance risk | **Population dashboards by design** — aggregates only, with minimum group sizes |

## How it works — in plain terms

1. **Wear** — Employees opt in and wear a supported smartwatch during work and rest. The companion phone app handles setup and personal feedback.

2. **Sense** — The watch quietly collects physiological and activity signals already available through modern health sensors: heart rhythm, movement, sleep, and related indicators. The system knows when a reading may be unreliable (for example, during intense movement) and adjusts accordingly.

3. **Understand** — On the device, those signals are woven into a personal wellbeing picture that evolves over days and weeks. The platform looks at how physical recovery, mental pressure, and social connection interact — because real fatigue rarely shows up in only one area.

4. **Act** — Individuals see guidance that helps them protect recovery and spot when strain is building. **HR and leadership see team-level trends** — never a live feed of one person’s private state — so you can intervene early with training, rostering, peer support, or occupational health outreach.

> **Privacy by architecture:** Individual wellbeing detail stays with the employee. Employer programmes receive **aggregated, anonymised workforce insights** subject to minimum group sizes and clear purpose limits. This is workforce intelligence, not surveillance.

## What HR and leadership get

- **Early warning** when strain is accumulating across a site, crew, or department — weeks before absence or incident data would show it  
- **Tri-domain dashboards** that reflect how safety-critical work actually breaks people: physical exhaustion, cognitive load, and isolation or team friction  
- **Evidence for programme decisions** — where to invest in EAP, roster design, supervisor training, or peer support  
- **Alignment with modern occupational health practice** (including ISO 45003-style whole-person wellbeing thinking) without claiming to replace medical or legal advice

## Real-world scenarios

**Rostering review** — HR notices a rising strain trend in a night-shift crew three weeks before a seasonal peak. Supervisors adjust handovers and add a recovery day before overtime ramps up. Absence in that team stays flat while neighbouring sites spike.

**Post-incident learning** — After a near-miss, safety leadership reviews anonymised population trends and sees that social-support scores had been declining for six weeks — not just sleep or heart-rate metrics. The follow-up programme targets team cohesion and supervisor check-ins, not only rest rules.

**Wellbeing programme ROI** — Instead of measuring EAP uptake alone, HR tracks whether organisation-wide strain indicators move after a manager-training rollout — giving the board a concrete story about prevention, not just cost of claims.

**Remote and rotational work** — Camp-based or fly-in-fly-out teams get continuous signal without relying on self-report in environments where admitting strain can feel career-limiting.
